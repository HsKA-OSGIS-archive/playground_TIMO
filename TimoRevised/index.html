    About Karlsruhe

    Karlsruhe was founded more than 300 years ago by Margrave Charles William (Karl) of Baden. Legend tells that the vision of a star-shaped city came to him in his sleep. The dream of Karlsruhe (“Charles’ Repose”) was born!
    Karlsruhe is the second-largest city of the German federal state of Baden-Württemberg after its capital of Stuttgart, and its 313,092 inhabitants make it the 21st largest city of Germany. On the right bank of the Rhine, the city lies near the French-German border, between the Mannheim/Ludwigshafen conurbation to the north, and the Strasbourg/Kehl conurbation to the south. It is the largest city of Baden, a historic region named after Hohenbaden Castle in the city of Baden-Baden.
    Karlsruhe is a tourist haven and boost of numerous tourist attractions such as the Botanischer Garten Karlsruhe, Durlacher Turmberg, Stadtgarten and so many other tourist attractions, its warm weather also makes it a delight all year round.
